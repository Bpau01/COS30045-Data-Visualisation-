However, Afghanistan repalced Malaysia among the top three countries after the reopening of the Australian border. Visualisation 2: Breakdown on Visa Type of Immigrants into Australia

Hover to see more

The donut chart displays the distribution of visa classifications, which include temporary visas, permanent visas, and others. Temporary visas have the greatest percentage of these categories, accounting for 58.2% of the total. The majority of temporary visa holders are higher education students, followed by visitors and persons on working holidays. Permanent visas account for the second-largest share, with the remaining visa types falling into the "others" group. This figure shows the visa distribution, emphasising the importance of temporary visas, especially for higher education students.

From the visualisation above we can notice that there is a large portion of immigrants arrive into Australia using different types of student visa

Visualisation 3: Relationship between International Students And The Total Number of Immigrants in Australia

The line chart depicts the number of immigrants and international students in Australia from 2012 to 2022. In 2018, the number of immigrants and international students in Australia was at its peak. Based on the line chart, it is evident that both lines exhibit a similar trend across time, with both experiencing increases and decreases. This demonstrates a correlation between the two variables and emphasises the country's reliance on immigration and international student enrolment.
